Al-Albānī's Manhaj Regarding the Hidden Defects of Ḥadīth (ʿIlal al-Ḥadīth) [Part 2]

Speaker: Shaykh al-Muḥaddith Abū ʿAlī al-Ḥasanī

English translation of the Shaykh’s speech:

Al-Albānī's Manhaj Regarding the Hidden Defects of Ḥadīth (ʿIlal al-Ḥadīth) [Part 2]

All praise is due to Allāh, and peace and blessings be upon the Messenger of Allāh. We are still discussing the manhaj of Al-Albānī regarding hidden defects (al-ʿilal).

Another ḥadīth example, of course, is the ḥadīth of ʿAbdul-Wārith bin Saʿīd: Ayyūb (meaning ash-Sakhtiyānī) narrated to us from Nāfiʿ from Ibn ʿUmar, who said: The Messenger of Allāh ﷺ said: “If only we left this door for women.” Nāfiʿ said: So Ibn ʿUmar never entered through it until he died.

Al-Imām Abū Dāwūd recorded this and said: “And someone other than ʿAbdul-Wārith reported it from ʿUmar, and that is more authentic.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

And Al-Imām Abū Dāwūd, when he narrated this ḥadīth, recorded immediately following it from the ḥadīth of Ismāʿīl bin ʿUlayyah from Ayyūb from Nāfiʿ, who said: ʿUmar bin al-Khaṭṭāb (رضي الله عنه) said its meaning, and it is more authentic. Meaning, this is an explicit text (tanṣīṣ) from Abū Dāwūd that it is mawqūf.

And despite this, what did Al-Albānī do? He said: “Ḍaʿīf.” Upon this narration, he said about it: “Ḍaʿīf.” So he did not merely suffice with contradicting Abū Dāwūd there and authenticating it when [Abū Dāwūd] had ruled it to be mawqūf; rather, he went further and weakened the narration that Abū Dāwūd explicitly stated and preferred as being mawqūf. So Allāh's help is sought.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Abū Isḥāq as-Sabīʿī from Al-Aswad bin Yazīd from ʿĀʾishah, who said: The Messenger of Allāh ﷺ used to sleep while he was junub (in a state of major ritual impurity) without touching water.

Abū Dāwūd recorded this, of course. Abū Dāwūd said: Al-Ḥasan bin ʿAlī al-Wāsiṭī narrated to us, he said: I heard Yazīd bin Hārūn saying: “This ḥadīth is an illusion/error (wahm),” meaning the ḥadīth of Abū Isḥāq.

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit: Al-Imām Abū Ḥātim said: I heard Naṣr bin ʿAlī saying: My father said: Shuʿbah said: “I have heard the ḥadīth of Abū Isḥāq that the Prophet ﷺ used to sleep while junub, but I avoid it.” And this is in Al-ʿIlal.

The point is: Al-Albānī does not respect the iʿlālāt (identifications of defects) of the ʾAʾimmah and gives them no weight. And I do not know of anyone from the people of knowledge except that he views this ḥadīth as weak and rejected (nakārah), but Allāh's help is sought against his authentications.

Another example: An-Nasāʾī said: Muḥammad bin Manṣūr informed us, he said: Ismāʿīl bin ʿUmar narrated to us, he said: Dāwūd bin Qays narrated to us from Muḥammad bin ʿAjlān from Yaḥyá bin Saʿīd from Anas bin Mālik that he saw the Messenger of Allāh praying on a donkey while he was riding towards Khaybar, and the Qiblah was behind him.

An-Nasāʾī said: “And the ḥadīth of Yaḥyá bin Saʿīd from Anas, the correct view is that it is mawqūf, and Allāh the Glorified and Exalted knows best.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit: This ḥadīth was declared defective by Al-Imām Al-Bukhārī, and he said: “Mālik and ʿAbdul-Wārith reported from Yaḥyá that he saw Anas, and it is more authentic,” meaning it is mawqūf upon Anas that he saw him praying on a donkey with the Qiblah behind him. And Ad-Dāraquṭnī said: “From Yaḥyá bin Saʿīd from Anas as mawqūf, and it is the correct view.”

So these are three ʾAʾimmah: Abū Dāwūd, Al-Bukhārī, Ad-Dāraquṭnī, and despite this, Al-Albānī says: “Ṣaḥīḥ.” So who is it that has a grasp of intellect in his head who leaves the iʿlālāt of these ḥuffāẓ ʾAʾimmah and proceeds to—the judgment of Al-Albānī? And Al-Albānī is a researcher (bāḥith), by the explicit statement of his senior students like Muqbil bin Hādī al-Wādiʿī, so Allāh's help is sought.

Another example is the ḥadīth of ʿAlī bin Mushir: Al-Aʿmash informed us from Abū Razīn and Abū Ṣāliḥ from Abū Hurayrah, who said: The Messenger of Allāh ﷺ said: “If a dog licks the vessel of one of you, let him spill it, then let him wash it seven times.”

An-Nasāʾī recorded this and said: “I do not know of anyone who corroborated (tābaʿa) ʿAlī bin Mushir upon his statement ‘let him spill it’.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit: This ḥadīth was recorded by Al-Imām Muslim in the book of Aṣ-Ṣaḥīḥ and he said following it: “And Muḥammad bin Ṣabāḥ narrated to me, Ismāʿīl bin Zakariyyā narrated to us from Al-Aʿmash with this isnād the likes of it, and he did not say ‘let him spill it’.”

And this is the angle of iʿlāl: that the addition of ‘let him spill it’ is not authentic, yet despite this, Al-Albānī authenticated it, so Allāh's help is sought.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Maymūn al-Qannād from Abū Qilābah from Muʿāwiyah bin Abī Sufyān (رضي الله عنه) that the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ forbade riding upon tiger skins and from wearing gold unless it is cut (into small pieces).

Abū Dāwūd recorded this, then said following it: “Abū Qilābah did not meet Muʿāwiyah.” Meaning, he intends to declare it defective due to interruption (inqiṭāʿ) and it being mursal.

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit here: Abū Ḥātim ar-Rāzī said: “Abū Qilābah did not hear from Muʿāwiyah bin Abī Sufyān.” I say—meaning me, Al-Ḥasanī—and Maymūn al-Qannād, his ḥadīth from Abū Qilābah is mursal, according to what Al-Bukhārī mentioned in At-Tārīkh al-Kabīr. And similarly, Al-Imām Aḥmad said in the biography of this Maymūn, meaning he narrated this ḥadīth and it is not well-known (laysa bi-maʿrūf). And similarly, Aḏ-Ḏahabī found it rejected (istankarah) as in Al-Mīzān.

Another example: Abū Dāwūd said: Aḥmad bin ʿAbdillāh al-Ġudānī narrated to us, Al-Walīd bin Muslim narrated to us, Saʿīd bin ʿAbdil-ʿAzīz narrated to us from Sulaymān bin Mūsá from Nāfiʿ, who said: Ibn ʿUmar heard a flute, so he placed his two fingers into his ears and turned away from the road, and he said to me: “O Nāfiʿ, do you hear anything?” I said: “No.” He said: So he removed his fingers from his ears and said: “I was with the Prophet ﷺ, and he heard something like this, so he did something like this.”

Abū ʿAlī al-Luʾluʾī, one of the transmitters of Sunan Abī Dāwūd, said: I heard Abū Dāwūd saying: “This is a munkar ḥadīth.”

And Abū Dāwūd narrated it from the ḥadīth of Maḥmūd bin Khālid, who said: My father narrated to us, Muṭʿim bin al-Miqdām narrated to us, Nāfiʿ narrated to us, who said: I was riding behind Ibn ʿUmar when he passed by a shepherd playing a flute... and he mentioned similarly. Abū Dāwūd said: “He inserted Sulaymān bin Mūsá between Muṭʿim and Nāfiʿ.”

And he narrated it from the ḥadīth of Aḥmad bin Ibrāhīm, who said: ʿAbdullāh bin Jaʿfar ar-Raqqī narrated to us, he said: Abū al-Malīḥ narrated to us from Maymūn from Nāfiʿ, who said: We were with Ibn ʿUmar and he heard the sound of a flute player... and he mentioned similarly. Abū Dāwūd said: “And this is more rejected (ankaruhā).”

Yet despite all of this—and Al-Albānī coming across the speech of Abū Dāwūd—he said regarding the first isnād: “Ṣaḥīḥ.” And regarding the second isnād, he said: “Ḥasan ṣaḥīḥ al-isnād.” And regarding the third isnād, about which Abū Dāwūd said “and this is more rejected”, Al-Albānī said: “This is ṣaḥīḥ al-isnād.” So he, with all of this, paid no attention to the statement of Al-Imām Abū Dāwūd, despite all this explanation and clarification for the iʿlāl of the ḥadīth.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Abū Qays from Huḏayl bin Shuraḥbīl from Al-Muġīrah bin Shuʿbah that the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ wiped over the socks (jawrabayn) and sandals (naʿlayn).

Of course, this was recorded by An-Nasāʾī. An-Nasāʾī said: “We do not know anyone who corroborated Abū Qays upon this narration, and the authentic report from Al-Muġīrah is that the Prophet ﷺ wiped over the leather socks (khuffayn).”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit: Al-Imām Abū Dāwūd likewise recorded this ḥadīth and said following it: “ʿAbdur-Raḥmān bin Mahdī would not narrate this ḥadīth because what is known from Al-Muġīrah is that the Prophet ﷺ wiped over the leather socks.” And Al-Bayhaqī relayed from ʿAbdur-Raḥmān al-Mahdī his statement to Sufyān ath-Thawrī: “If you had narrated to me the ḥadīth of Abū Qays from Huḏayl, I would not have accepted it from you.” So Sufyān said: “The ḥadīth is weak (ḍaʿīf), or flimsy (wāhin)”—or a word similar to it.

And likewise, Al-Bayhaqī relayed from Ibn Maʿīn that he said: “The people, all of them, narrate it as being over the leather socks (khuffayn) except Abū Qays.” And similarly, it was relayed from Ibn al-Madīnī that he said: “The ḥadīth of Al-Muġīrah regarding wiping was narrated from Al-Muġīrah by the people of Al-Madīnah, the people of Al-Kūfah, and the people of Al-Baṣrah, and it was narrated by Huḏayl bin Shuraḥbīl, except that he said: ‘and he wiped over the socks (jawrabayn)’, and thereby contradicted the people.” And his weakening of this report was also relayed from Muslim bin al-Ḥajjāj, and that he said: “Abū Qays al-Awdī and Huḏayl bin Shuraḥbīl cannot bear this [weight] alongside their contradiction of the prominent scholars who narrated this ḥadīth from Al-Muġīrah and said: ‘he wiped over the leather socks’.”

This is one of the problematic issues with Al-Albānī: he does not pay attention to contradiction (al-Mukhālafah). The outward appearance of the isnād according to him is authentic, a narrator is acceptable (maqbūl)—meaning, not merely thiqah (trustworthy), but acceptable according to his own conditions—that's it, the ḥadīth is authentic. Regardless of whether the ʾAʾimmah declared it defective or did not declare it defective, whether he came across the ʿillah or did not come across it. So Allāh's help is sought.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Ibrāhīm bin ʿUthmān from Al-Ḥakam from Miqsam from Ibn ʿAbbās that the Prophet ﷺ recited the Opening of the Book (Fātiḥat al-Kitāb) over a funeral.

At-Tirmiḏī recorded this and said following it: “The ḥadīth of Ibn ʿAbbās is a ḥadīth whose isnād is not that strong. Ibrāhīm bin ʿUthmān is Abū Shaybah al-Wāsiṭī, and he is rejected in ḥadīth (munkar al-ḥadīth). And the authentic report from Ibn ʿAbbās is his statement: ‘From the Sunnah is to recite the Opening of the Book over the funeral’.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “A ṣaḥīḥ ḥadīth.”

A benefit here: The ḥadīth was recorded by Ibn ʿAdī among the rejected narrations (manākīr) of Ibrāhīm bin ʿUthmān Abū Shaybah al-ʿAbsī, and he said: “Abū Shaybah has aḥādīth that are not valid (ġayr ṣāliḥah) other than what I have mentioned from Al-Ḥakam and from others, and he is weak (ḍaʿīf) based on what I have clarified.” And likewise, Ad-Dāraquṭnī recorded it in Al-Afrād and said: “This is a ġarīb (isolated) ḥadīth from the ḥadīth of Al-Ḥakam bin ʿUtaybah from Miqsam from Ibn ʿAbbās; Abū Shaybah Ibrāhīm bin ʿUthmān was isolated (tafarrada bih) in narrating it from him.”

Of course, when the ʾAʾimmah make a statement like this, they intend by it the unacceptability (nakārah) of the ḥadīth, and this is apparent to anyone who traces their foundational principles. But Al-Albānī does not acknowledge this taʿlīl and gives it no weight. So Allāh's help is sought.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Ṣadaqah bin Mūsá ad-Daqīqī: Abū ʿImrān al-Jawnī narrated to us from Anas bin Mālik, who said: The Messenger of Allāh ﷺ set a time limit for us regarding shaving the pubic hair, trimming the nails, clipping the mustache, and plucking the armpit hair: once every forty days.

Of course, Abū Dāwūd recorded this and said: “And Jaʿfar bin Sulaymān narrated it from Abū ʿImrān from Anas, and he did not mention the Prophet ﷺ; he said: ‘A time limit was set for us...’ and this is more authentic.”

And similarly, At-Tirmiḏī recorded it with the wording “A time limit was set for us,” and said: “This is more authentic than the first ḥadīth, and Ṣadaqah bin Mūsá is not considered a ḥāfiẓ according to them.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit: Al-ʿUqaylī said in Aḍ-Ḍuʿafāʾ after recording it among the rejected narrations (munkarāt) of Ṣadaqah bin Mūsá ad-Daqīqī, he said: “And he is not corroborated upon elevating it (rafʿih)”—meaning, it is mawqūf.

These examples are from what he called his books Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan Abī Dāwūd, Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan at-Tirmiḏī, and Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan an-Nasāʾī. Meaning, while he was dividing these books, he should have at least not mentioned these aḥādīth which the ʾAʾimmah declared defective and explicitly stated their weakness, and instead authenticated what is besides them, or what they remained silent about from what he views as authentic. So Allāh's help is sought.

And from what I came across in what is called As-Silsilah aṣ-Ṣaḥīḥah—his series is Silsilat al-Aḥādīth aṣ-Ṣaḥīḥah, as he named it—among them are aḥādīth where he explicitly mentions the iʿlālāt of the ʾAʾimmah and explicitly states his rejection of them.

Among them is the ḥadīth: “Paradise has one hundred levels; between every two levels is a distance of a year's journey, like [the distance] between the heaven and the earth, and Al-Firdaws is the highest of them. And from it gush forth the four rivers, and the Throne is above it. So if you ask Allāh, ask Him for Al-Firdaws.”

He said: “And At-Tirmiḏī declared it defective due to contradiction (mukhālafah) and said: ‘And thus Hishām bin Saʿd narrated this ḥadīth from Zayd bin Aslam from ʿAṭāʾ bin Yasār from Muʿāḏ bin Jabal, and this according to me is more authentic than the ḥadīth of Hammām from Zayd bin Aslam from ʿAṭāʾ bin Yasār from ʿUbādah bin aṣ-Ṣāmit. And ʿUbādah did not meet Muʿāḏ bin Jabal; Muʿāḏ died early, he died during the caliphate of ʿUmar.’”

Then he—meaning Al-Albānī—said: “I say,” after pushing away this ʿillah. Let us look now at how he pushes away the ʿilal so that the student of knowledge becomes aware of the reality of Al-Albānī’s action. Al-Albānī said: “Hammām bin Yaḥyá is trustworthy (thiqah), utilized as evidence in Aṣ-Ṣaḥīḥ, so it is possible that ʿAṭāʾ had two chains of transmission for it: one from ʿUbādah which he memorized himself, and the other from Muʿāḏ which the group memorized, so there is no contradiction.” End of his speech.

I say: Subḥān Allāh, does he not realize that the ḥuffāẓ, when they declare a ḥadīth defective, its meaning according to them is that its narrator had an illusion (wahm) in it, erred in it, and one isnād became intertwined with another isnād for him, and that the isnād which they used to declare it defective is the preserved one (maḥfūẓ)?

And if they had this possibility which Al-Albānī mentioned—that it had two isnāds for it, or that the ḥadīth had more than one isnād—this would not have escaped them, and they would have stated it. They are the most expert of people and the most knowledgeable of people.

The point is that this is one of the major problems of Al-Albānī: he does not understand the meaning of iʿlāl according to the ʾAʾimmah, nor the foundational essence of iʿlāl and their intent behind it. So Allāh's help is sought.
