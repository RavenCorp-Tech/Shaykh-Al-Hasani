Al-Albānī's Manhaj Regarding the Hidden Defects of Ḥadīth (ʿIlal al-Ḥadīth) [Part 1]

Speaker: Shaykh al-Muḥaddith Abū ʿAlī al-Ḥasanī

English translation of the Shaykh’s speech:

Al-Albānī's Manhaj Regarding the Hidden Defects of Ḥadīth (ʿIlal al-Ḥadīth)

All praise is due to Allāh, and peace and blessings be upon the Messenger of Allāh. To proceed:

We are still in the process of clarifying the manhaj of Shaykh Al-Albānī in the sciences of ḥadīth, authentication (at-taṣḥīḥ), weakening (at-taḍʿīf), judging narrators, extracting ḥadīth (at-takhrīj), and dealing with the foundational principles of ḥadīth.

Today’s discussion is about Al-Albānī’s methodology concerning hidden defects (ʿilal); did Al-Albānī master this science? Did he pay attention to al-ʿilal? Did he give any weight to the defect-identifications (iʿlālāt) of the ʾAʾimmah? All of this, in shāʾ Allāh, is what we will clarify.

However, I want to mention a very important issue: Shaykh Al-Albānī was not from the ḥuffāẓ (memorizers/masters of ḥadīth). For this reason, many ʿilal escaped him, regardless of whether he came across a critical statement (iʿlāl) of an Imām here or not. Meaning: if he came across a chain of transmission (isnād) and did not come across the iʿlāl of one of the ʾAʾimmah, it would escape him.

  • For example, the aḥādīth of Jaʿfar bin Burqān from Az-Zuhrī are declared defective by the ḥuffāẓ.
  • Like the aḥādīth of Asbāṭ bin Naṣr al-Kūfī from Simāk bin Ḥarb.
  • The ḥadīth of Ayyūb bin Abī Tamīmah as-Sakhtiyānī from Yaḥyá bin Abī Kathīr.
  • Like the ḥadīth of Jarīr bin Ḥāzim from Qatādah.
  • Like the ḥadīth of Ḥabīb bin Abī Thābit from ʿAṭāʾ.
  • Like the aḥādīth of Sufyān bin ʿUyaynah from the Kūfans.
  • Like the aḥādīth of ʿAbdullāh bin Wahb from Ibn Jurayj.

These escape Shaykh Al-Albānī.

Likewise, many of the iʿlālāt regarding interruptions (al-inqiṭāʿ) escape him, such as the narration of Al-Aʿmash, for example, from Ṭalḥah bin Nāfiʿ. Like the narration of ʿAṭāʾ bin Abī Rabāḥ from Ibn ʿUmar or from Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī. Or the narration of Ibrāhīm bin Yazīd an-Nakhaʿī from ʿĀʾishah, and so on.

Al-Albānī is not a ḥāfiẓ. And if a man is not a ḥāfiẓ—especially regarding the foundations of al-ʿilal—then how can he speak on the sciences of ḥadīth, stretch forth his hand, and establish his pen to extract aḥādīth, authenticate, and weaken?

The pivot and secret of the science of ḥadīth is the science of al-ʿilal. The science of al-ʿilal oversees and dominates all the sciences of ḥadīth.

We are not, in this series, speaking about Al-Albānī’s lack of precision in al-ʿilal in a general sense. No. Rather, we will speak about a very important point that will serve as our evidence to clarify the reality of Al-Albānī in dealing with al-ʿilal, and that he did not give any weight to the iʿlālāt of the ʾAʾimmah.

Here is a group of aḥādīth where Al-Albānī actually came across the iʿlālāt of the ʾAʾimmah. We will not mention now aḥādīth where Al-Albānī did not come across the iʿlālāt of the ʾAʾimmah, because a fanatic (mutaʿaṣṣib) might come to us and say, “He did not come across the ʿillah,” or something similar—even though we do not excuse him, because he ought to have researched since he is not a ḥāfiẓ. But here, we bring what he DID come across, yet openly contradicted.

He wrote books such as what is called Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan Abī Dāwūd, Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan at-Tirmiḏī, Ṣaḥīḥ Sunan an-Nasāʾī. Here, we will stop at aḥādīth which the ḥuffāẓ explicitly declared defective—explicitly, not implicitly—yet despite this, he judged them to be ṣaḥīḥ (authentic).

These books, of course, we are not currently discussing the division of these books into ṣaḥīḥ and ḍaʿīf and his actual overstepping against the books of the ʾAʾimmah. This, in shāʾ Allāh, will have its own discussion that we will come to regarding the issue of Al-Albānī dividing the books of Sunan and his overstepping (iftiʾāt) against the ʾAʾimmah.

The point and purpose here is to bring aḥādīth where Al-Albānī came across the iʿlālāt of the ʾAʾimmah, yet despite this, he rejected them, giving no weight to the ʾAʾimmah nor to what they declared defective.

The important question before giving examples: Why did Al-Albānī do this? Why does Al-Albānī do this? Why does he come across the iʿlāl of a brilliant ḥāfiẓ Imām, or a group of ʾAʾimmah—not just one, as we will see, in shāʾ Allāh—and despite this, he rejects it, gives it no weight, and pays no attention to it? Why does he do this?

There is a secret. The secret is that Al-Albānī is obsessed with authentication. He has a passion for authenticating aḥādīth. He barely finds an isnād whose outward appearance is authentic except that he authenticates it. And if he comes up empty-handed from its outward appearance, he builds upon the issue of corroborating chains (mutābaʿāt), as we presented previously regarding the issue of al-Mutābaʿāt and ash-Shawāhid.

The point of all this is that Al-Albānī does all of this because he wants to authenticate the aḥādīth, push them through, and pass them along, especially if the ḥadīth aligns with his fiqh or his desired outcome.

The examples that we are about to present are by way of indication, not an exhaustive restriction:

For example, the ḥadīth of Sufyān from Abū Rawq from Ibrāhīm at-Taymī from ʿĀʾishah that the Prophet ﷺ kissed her and did not perform wuḍūʾ.

Abū Dāwūd recorded this in As-Sunan, and Abū Dāwūd said following it: “It is mursal. Ibrāhīm at-Taymī did not hear from ʿĀʾishah. Ibrāhīm at-Taymī died before reaching forty years of age, and his kunyah was Abū Asmāʾ.”

And At-Tirmiḏī said after narrating it: “And it has been narrated from Ibrāhīm at-Taymī from ʿĀʾishah that the Prophet ﷺ kissed her and did not perform wuḍūʾ, and this is not authentic either. We do not know of Ibrāhīm at-Taymī hearing from ʿĀʾishah, and nothing is authentic from the Prophet ﷺ in this chapter.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “Ṣaḥīḥ.”

Of course, I am not here to comment or clarify the angle of refutation against Al-Albānī for his lack of—meaning—respect for the taʿlīl (declaring defective) of the ʾAʾimmah and pushing forward with authentication contrary to what they said. No, this is a very lengthy matter, and its proper place is at-Takhrīj. I will only suffice with clarifying his rejection and contradiction of the ḥuffāẓ ʾAʾimmah.

Another example is the ḥadīth of ʿAbdullāh bin Nāfiʿ from Al-Layth bin Saʿd from Bakr bin Sawādah from ʿAṭāʾ bin Yasār from Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī, who said: Two men went out on a journey, and the time for prayer arrived while they had no water with them. So they performed tayammum with clean earth and prayed. Then they found water during the time [of that prayer]. So one of them repeated the prayer and the wuḍūʾ, and the other did not repeat. Then they came to the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ and mentioned that to him. So he said to the one who did not repeat: “You have struck the Sunnah, and your prayer sufficed you.” And he said to the one who performed wuḍūʾ and repeated: “For you is the reward twice over.”

The Imām and Ḥāfiẓ Abū Dāwūd said: “Someone other than Ibn Nāfiʿ narrates it from Al-Layth from ʿAmīrah bin Abī Nājiyah from Bakr bin Sawādah from ʿAṭāʾ bin Yasār from the Prophet ﷺ.”

And he said: “And the mention of Abū Saʿīd al-Khudrī in this ḥadīth is not connected (laysa bi-muttaṣil), and it is mursal.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “This is a ṣaḥīḥ ḥadīth.”

A benefit: Naturally, the ḥadīth was recorded by Aṭ-Ṭabarānī in Al-Awsaṭ, and he said: “No one narrated this ḥadīth from Al-Layth with a connected isnād except ʿAbdullāh.”

And Al-Imām Ad-Dāraquṭnī said in As-Sunan: “ʿAbdullāh bin Nāfiʿ was isolated (tafarrada bih) from Al-Layth with this isnād as connected, and Ibn al-Mubārak and others contradicted him.” Meaning, Imām Ad-Dāraquṭnī indicates that they narrated it as mursal.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Jaʿfar bin Burqān from Az-Zuhrī from Sālim from his father, who said: The Messenger of Allāh ﷺ forbade two types of eating: sitting at a table where wine is drunk, and a man eating while lying flat on his stomach.

Al-Imām Abū Dāwūd recorded this in As-Sunan and said: “Jaʿfar did not hear this ḥadīth from Az-Zuhrī, and it is munkar (rejected).”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī gave no weight to the iʿlāl of Imām Abū Dāwūd and said: “It is ṣaḥīḥ.”

A benefit: The ḥadīth came in Al-ʿIlal—the ʿIlal of Ibn Abī Ḥātim. Abū Ḥātim said: “This ḥadīth is a mistake. They narrate it from Jaʿfar from a man from Az-Zuhrī like this. This is not from the authentic ḥadīth of Az-Zuhrī, and it is fabricated (muftaʿal); it is not from the ḥadīth of the trustworthy narrators (ath-Thiqāt).”

Meaning, it is fabricated (mawḍūʿ), yet despite this, Al-Albānī authenticates it.

It would have sufficed Al-Albānī, had he been a ḥāfiẓ of al-ʿilal, to know that an ʿillah like the one in this ḥadīth does not even require effort or research. That is because the ḥuffāẓ explicitly stated that the narration of Jaʿfar bin Burqān from Az-Zuhrī is weak, as Yaḥyá bin Maʿīn said. And An-Nasāʾī said: “He is not strong regarding Az-Zuhrī specifically.” And Ad-Dāraquṭnī said: “Jaʿfar was unlettered (ummī). There is some illusion (wahm) in his memory, and especially in his aḥādīth from Az-Zuhrī.”

So an ʿillah like this—meaning, we don’t even need to research it. Any student of knowledge who possesses memorization of al-ʿilal would simply stop at it and declare this ḥadīth weak.

Another example is the ḥadīth of Abū Marḥūm ʿAbdir-Raḥīm bin Maymūn from Sahl bin Muʿāḏ bin Anas al-Juhanī from his father, that the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ said: “Whoever gives for Allāh, withholds for Allāh, loves for Allāh, hates for Allāh, and marries for Allāh, then he has perfected faith.”

Al-Imām At-Tirmiḏī recorded this and said after recording it: “This is a munkar ḥadīth.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “This is a ḥasan ḥadīth.”

And here I point out: if Al-Albānī says “ṣaḥīḥ”, it is somewhat less severe than him saying “ḥasan”. For if he says a ḥadīth is “ḥasan”, then this represents a more severe leniency (tasāhul) and a pushing away of the iʿlāl, or his ignorance of the origin of the ʿillah, the interruption (inqiṭāʿ), or the illusion (wahm) of the narrators. And this [example] is of this sort.

A benefit: Naturally, regarding Abū Marḥūm ʿAbdir-Raḥīm bin Maymūn, it would have sufficed Al-Albānī to at least look into his biographical entry (tarjamah). Meaning, Ibn Maʿīn says about him: “Weak in ḥadīth (ḍaʿīf al-ḥadīth).” Abū Ḥātim says: “His ḥadīth is written down, but it is not used as evidence.”

And the ḥadīth, of course, was corroborated (tābaʿahu) by Zabbān bin Fāʾid from Sahl bin Muʿāḏ. And this Sahl bin Muʿāḏ inherently has weakness in him, or is weak, as Imām Ibn Maʿīn said. And Ibn Ḥibbān said: “His ḥadīth is not given consideration if it is from the narration of Zabbān bin Fāʾid.” And he said in Aḍ-Ḍuʿafāʾ: “And he is very munkar in ḥadīth. I do not know if the confusion (takhlīṭ) in his ḥadīth occurred from him or from Zabbān. If it was from either of them, then the reports he narrated are dropped (sāqiṭah). And this was only ambiguous because the narrator from Sahl was Zabbān, except for a thing after a thing. And Zabbān is nothing (laysa bi-shayʾ).”

So at the very least, if the ʿillah of the ḥadīth is hidden from him, he should look into the biographies (tarājim). And even regarding the biographies—the biographies of the narrators—he sometimes, as is famous about him, relies on Taqrīb at-Tahḏīb by Ibn Ḥajr. Yet Taqrīb at-Tahḏīb by Ibn Ḥajr—Ibn Ḥajr himself was not satisfied with it.

The point is another example: the ḥadīth of Muṣʿab bin Thābit from Muḥammad bin al-Munkadir from Jābir bin ʿAbdullāh, who said: A thief was brought to the Messenger of Allāh ﷺ. He said: “Kill him.” They said: “O Messenger of Allāh, he only stole.” He said: “Cut him [his hand].” So he was cut.

Then he was brought a second time, and he said: “Kill him.” They said: “O Messenger of Allāh, he only stole.” He said: “Cut him.” So he was cut. Then he was brought a third time, and he said: “Kill him.” They said: “O Messenger of Allāh, he only stole.” He said: “Cut him.” Then he was brought a fourth time, and he said: “Kill him.” They said: “O Messenger of Allāh, he only stole.” He said: “Cut him.” Then he was brought a fifth time, and he said: “Kill him.”

Jābir said: So we took him away to a camel enclosure, and we charged at him. He lay flat on his back, then bared his teeth/grimaced (kashshara) with his hands and feet, so the camels scattered. Then they charged at him a second time, and he did the same. Then they charged at him a third time, so we pelted him with stones and killed him. Then we threw him into a well and threw stones upon him.

Al-Imām An-Nasāʾī recorded this, and Al-Imām An-Nasāʾī said: “And this is a munkar ḥadīth. And Muṣʿab bin Thābit is not strong (laysa bi-al-qawī) in ḥadīth, and Allāh the Exalted knows best.”

And An-Nasāʾī added, of course, in Al-Kubrá: “And I do not know of any ṣaḥīḥ ḥadīth from the Prophet ﷺ in this chapter.”

Yet despite this, Al-Albānī said: “This is a ṣaḥīḥ ḥadīth.”

A benefit: Al-Imām Aṭ-Ṭabarānī likewise recorded it in Al-Muʿjam al-Awsaṭ and said: “No one narrated this ḥadīth from Muḥammad bin al-Munkadir except Muṣʿab,” intending by that to declare it munkar, and Allāh knows best.
